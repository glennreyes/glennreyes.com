I've always been curious about how things work and how they can be refined. That curiosity still drives my work today, but the questions have shifted. I now spend a lot of time building AI-native products, exploring how interfaces change when users express intent instead of clicking through fixed screens.

I [speak](/talks) at conferences and meetups about React, MCP, and Generative UI, and I run [workshops](/workshops) for teams and communities that want to build more adaptive, interactive frontend systems. I enjoy creating products that feel clear, fast, and deeply considered, especially when design systems, developer experience, and emerging platform capabilities need to work together.

Much of what excites me right now lives at the intersection of AI and frontend engineering: MCP-powered products, context-aware user interfaces, and the patterns that help teams build them in a way that stays maintainable.

If you’d like to see the gear and apps I use every day, have a look at my [uses page](/uses).

When I’m not coding, I’m usually with the people closest to me, playing guitar, or spending time outdoors. I love diving, and I stay active with [cycling, swimming, and running](https://www.strava.com/athletes/14875783).
